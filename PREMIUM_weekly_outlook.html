Premium Weekly Macro Thesis

The week of 18 May 2026 opens under a fully inverted macro regime. Two weeks ago the thesis was "Dollar weakness — Fed cuts by September." That thesis is dead. Hot CPI at 3.2%, accelerating PPI, Strait of Hormuz closure, and a Trump-Xi summit breakdown have collectively repriced the Fed from cut to hike. CME FedWatch now prices a rate hike by March 2027 with over 50% probability of a move before year-end 2026.

DXY closed Friday at 99.39 — its highest level since April 2026, up nearly 1% on the week. The path of least resistance is now toward 100.00 and above. The key question this week: do the FOMC Minutes on Tuesday confirm the hawkish repricing? If yes, DXY breaks 100 and EUR/USD tests 1.1480. If no — if the minutes show a divided committee — expect a short-term USD pullback to 98.50, which is your buy-the-dip opportunity.

Gold is the most interesting market to watch. Net long positioning remains elevated at 186K contracts — those longs are trapped above $4,600. As real yields rise and the Dollar strengthens, those longs will be force-sold. Every bounce in Gold toward $4,580–$4,620 is a selling opportunity. The next major support cluster is $4,380–$4,420.

Elijah's Premium Weekly Thesis
This is a single-narrative week — Dollar strength is the only trade. Every setup I am watching is a USD-long or a commodity/risk-short.
