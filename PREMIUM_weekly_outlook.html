Premium Weekly Macro Thesis

The week of 8 June 2026 opens in the shadow of Friday's seismic NFP print. The US economy added 172,000 jobs in May — more than double the 85,000 consensus — and prior months were revised upward by a combined 93,000 additional jobs. The unemployment rate held at 4.3%, its lowest since August, and annual wage growth came in at a contained 3.4%. This is the most labour market-bullish data set of 2026, and the market reaction was instant: DXY surged toward the 100.00 threshold, EUR/USD cratered through 1.15, and Gold dropped to $4,366 — its lowest level since March 2026 and down 12% from its peak. The Dollar is not just bid. It is dominant.

The implications compound this week. Wednesday brings US CPI for May and Thursday the ECB rate decision — two events that together define the EUR/USD trajectory for June. The CPI print follows April's 3.8% headline; if May CPI stays sticky above 3.5%, the FOMC's June 16–17 meeting — Chair Warsh's first — becomes live for hawkish signalling. The ECB, meanwhile, is expected to hike to 2.40% on Thursday, facing its own inflation challenge with eurozone CPI at 3.0%. A hike paired with dovish forward guidance relative to a hawkish-tilting Fed widens the rate differential and sends EUR/USD through 1.1400. This week's calendar is as dense as the year gets.

Gold is now in a confirmed downtrend. The $4,493–$4,540 zone — defined in last week's report as the critical support cluster — broke clean on Friday. XAU/USD is now trading below yearly support at $4,366, with the RSI at a neutral-to-bearish 43. The next major support sits at $4,185. The Iran situation remains fluid: reports of progress toward a Strait of Hormuz deal sent oil down more than 3% on Friday. Any confirmed peace deal would remove the inflation premium entirely, hammering Gold further and reducing the safe-haven bid for both JPY and CHF. The USD trade is accelerating — the question is whether to chase or wait for retrace.
