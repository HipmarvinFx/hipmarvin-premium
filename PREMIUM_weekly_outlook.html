Premium Weekly Macro Thesis

The week of 25 May 2026 opens under a regime-transition moment. The Federal Reserve has a new chairman — Kevin Warsh was sworn in on 22 May, replacing Powell. Warsh is known as a structural hawk who has publicly questioned the Fed's inflation-fighting credibility. Markets are watching his first public communications with acute attention. Combined with US CPI printing at 3.8% (May 12) and a 55% probability now priced for at least one Fed hike by October, the Dollar retains its structural bid heading into this week.

DXY closed Friday at 99.32 — range-bound but supported above 99.00. The critical test this week is Thursday's double-header: GDP 2nd Release and the PCE Deflator — the Fed's preferred inflation gauge. If PCE comes in hot (above 3.5% YoY), the hike trade gets fully repriced and DXY breaks 100. If PCE surprises softer, we get a brief USD dip that is a buy opportunity. This is the week's single most important data point. EUR/USD is trapped in a 1.16–1.18 range; a hot PCE breaks 1.16 and opens 1.1480.

Gold closed Friday at $4,517 — the second consecutive weekly decline. The US-Iran situation remains unresolved: Rubio reported "slight progress" in talks but no deal, and Iran's Supreme Leader ordered enriched uranium stockpiles to remain in-country, complicating any agreement. Elevated oil, elevated inflation expectations, and a newly hawkish Fed chair are a toxic cocktail for Gold. Net long positioning in XAU/USD futures remains dangerously elevated. The path lower continues — target $4,380–$4,420 for the week.
