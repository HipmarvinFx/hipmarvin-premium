Week in Review — Premium Desk

The week ending 16 May 2026 delivered a significant regime shift: the Dollar is back. Both CPI and PPI came in hot — energy shock driven by the Strait of Hormuz closure and escalating US-Iran tensions pushed inflation higher than consensus expected. Markets have completely repriced Fed expectations. There will be no cuts in 2026. Traders are now fully pricing one rate hike by March 2027, with a 50%+ probability of a move before year-end 2026.

DXY closed the week at 99.39 — up nearly 1% on the week and at its highest level since April 2026. Gold sold off sharply from above $4,700 earlier in the month to $4,533 as real yields climbed and safe-haven flows shifted toward the Dollar. EUR/USD lost 1.6% on the week, and GBP/USD had its worst week in months, down around 2%.

The macro narrative has inverted. We are no longer in a Dollar-weakness environment. USD strength is now the base case until data changes. Every trade this week must be filtered through that lens.

Elijah's Premium Take — 18 May 2026
This is a market that punished complacency. Anyone still long EUR/USD or short DXY from the prior week's narrative got squeezed hard. The Strait of Hormuz closure is the X-factor nobody modelled correctly. Energy prices up = US CPI up = Fed hawkish = Dollar up. This chain is now the dominant theme going into the week of 18 May. I am not chasing USD longs here — DXY at 99.39 is extended short-term. I am waiting for a pullback to 98.50–98.80 to re-enter. Patience over FOMO.

 PPI accelerated at its fastest pace since 2022 in April. This confirmed the CPI story — inflation is re-accelerating, not slowing. Beat
DXY extended its gains. EUR/USD broke below 1.1700, GBP/USD broke below 1.3600. Gold tested $4,480 intraday.
US Retail Sales — 15 May

Not a Dollar catalyst but did not undermine the hawkish Fed narrative either. USD held its gains.
Trump-Xi Summit — 15–16 May
 Summit ended without major agreements. Xi warned Taiwan could become a source of clashes. Risk sentiment deteriorated Friday. AUD/USD fell the most of the majors — down nearly 1% on the day. Safe-haven flows into USD accelerated into the weekend close.
