Friday Week Close — Japan CPI · Iran Stalemate · USD Holds Six-Week High

Friday 22 May opens in Asian trade with the Dollar perched near a six-week high, having survived a volatile overnight session driven by conflicting signals on the US-Iran peace deal. DXY stands at 99.24 — not far from Wednesday's peak of 99.515. The Iran deal has not materialised. Washington and Tehran remain stuck on opposing stances over uranium enrichment levels and control of the Strait of Hormuz. US Secretary of State Rubio offered cautious optimism — "some good signs" — but Polymarket's probability of a deal by May 22 collapsed from 7% to 4% this week. The ceasefire is extended, the naval blockade continues, and the structural USD bull case from FOMC Minutes remains fully intact.

This morning's dominant data point: Japan's National Core CPI rose just 1.4% YoY in April — the slowest annual pace since March 2022, missing the 1.7% forecast sharply and dropping from 1.8% in March. Government education subsidies drove the weakness. USD/JPY is attracting dip-buyers in the Asian session on this print, recovering toward 159.10 after yesterday's pullback. However, analysts and BoJ watchers expect the data to prove transitory — Iran war energy costs will feed back into Japanese prices from June onwards. The BoJ is still widely expected to raise its policy rate from 0.75% to 1.0% at the June 15–16 meeting.

Gold is under fresh selling pressure in Asian trade, declining toward $4,519 — the second straight day of losses for the metal. The USD remaining near its six-week high, combined with mixed Iran signals that fail to create a clean safe-haven bid, keeps Gold pinned. AUD/USD is oscillating around 0.7145, unable to attract meaningful buyers as the combined headwinds of the dismal Thursday jobs report and hawkish Fed rate hike expectations override any Iran peace optimism. The week ends today — US markets face a shortened Friday session, with full Monday closure for Memorial Day.

Week in Review — W/C 18 May 2026 · Full Summary

This was one of the cleanest macro weeks in recent memory. Every major event aligned with the USD bullish thesis established in Sunday's Premium Outlook. Tuesday through Wednesday saw the structural USD rally resume as bond yields held elevated and the Warsh-era Fed took shape. Wednesday's FOMC Minutes (April 28–29) were the week's centrepiece: an 8–4 vote with three formal dissenters against the easing bias, a hawkish axis of Hammack, Kashkari, and Logan confirmed, and a committee that is actively debating rate hikes, not cuts.

GBP ended the week up 0.8% — a recovery from last week's 2%+ fall — but remains structurally bearish with the BoE June 18 cut now a 60%+ probability.

The week's three defining moments: (1) Moody's downgrade revealing that bond vigilantes, not the Fed, are now the dominant force in rates markets. (2) FOMC Minutes confirming the hawkish pivot is institutional, not just rhetoric. (3) AUD/USD breaking below 0.7100 on the Australia jobs disaster — the cleanest trade of the week from entry to target.
