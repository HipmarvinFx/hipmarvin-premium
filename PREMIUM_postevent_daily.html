Week in Review — Premium Desk

The week ending 16 May 2026 has confirmed what I flagged in the prior week's premium outlook: the inflation regime is back, and the Dollar is the primary beneficiary. April CPI landed at +0.6% MoM and +3.8% YoY — above the +3.7% consensus. The energy component surged +3.8% for the month, driven by the ongoing US naval blockade of Iran and elevated Strait of Hormuz transit risk. Core CPI at +0.4% MoM was also above expectations, showing energy price gains are now bleeding into the broader price basket.

PPI confirmed the picture with force: final demand producer prices rose +1.4% in April, pushing the annual rate to +6.0% YoY — a pace not seen since 2022. Services PPI +1.2%, goods PPI +2.0%. This is not a transitory shock. The CME FedWatch tool now prices a 97%+ probability of rates holding at 3.50–3.75% through June. Rate cuts have been completely priced out. The USD regime shift is now a confirmed macro fact, not a trade thesis.

DXY closed Friday at 99.35 — within striking distance of the psychologically critical 100.00 handle. EUR/USD dropped to fresh six-week lows around 1.1484 intraday before recovering to 1.1613. GBP/USD found support above 1.3260 but Cable remains structurally compromised. USD/JPY pushed to 158.90 in the Asian session Tuesday — just 1.10 away from the BoJ's 160.00 intervention line. Gold has fallen sharply from the $4,700+ area earlier this month, trading to a 1.5-month low near $4,440. Every major price move this week has one common denominator: the Dollar.
